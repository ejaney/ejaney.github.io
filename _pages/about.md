How do the spaces we navigate shape how we see one another?

I am a PhD student in Psychology at the University of Chicago, investigating the intersection of race, space, and social cognition. My research employs social, cognitive, cultural, and environmental approaches to understand how physical and systemic environments shape human perception and interaction. Based in the [Environmental Neuroscience Lab](https://voices.uchicago.edu/bermanlab/) and mentored by Dr. Marc Berman, my interdisciplinary work is also enriched by affiliations with the [Tracing Historical Roots of Equity and Disparity Lab](https://www.kyshiahenderson.com/) Lab and the [Couples’ Experiences with Diversity and Adversity in Relationships Lab](https://lydiaemery.com/) Lab.

Previously, I received a B.S. in Psychology from the University of Iowa where I worked as a research assistant in the [Blumberg Lab](https://blumberg.lab.uiowa.edu/). Following graduation, I worked as the lab manager of the Environmental Neuroscience Lab at the University of Chicago.
